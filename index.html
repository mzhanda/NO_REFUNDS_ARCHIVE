Trait System

What Makes Each Receipt Different

Each NFT is built from archive traits: a store series, a paper condition, a stamp, a handwritten note, and sometimes a hidden narrative line. The archive never rewrites the receipt. It only records what surrounded it.

Store Series

The closed place the receipt came from: diner, motel, video counter, record basement, convenience store, or pawn shop.

Base Model

The way the receipt is found: on a counter, under glass, in a collage, on wet pavement, or sealed in an archive sleeve.

Paper Condition

Faded, torn, folded, stained, warped, burned at the corner, or just barely readable.

Stamps

FINAL SALE, NO REFUNDS, CLOSED, AFTER HOURS, LAST COPY, CLAIM CLOSED, and other archive marks.

Handwriting

Room numbers, table numbers, claim codes, late-night initials, and notes that explain everything or nothing at all.

Props

Keys, tokens, cassette labels, pawn tickets, sugar packets, map scraps, bottle caps, and broken objects.

Closed Places

The Six Closed Places

Every receipt in the archive belongs to one of six fictional places. Each place leaves behind its own kind of proof.

Midnight Diner

Open 24 Hours. Closed forever.

Late checks, night specials, booth numbers, and things left unsaid.

Night Owl Video

Rental counter after midnight.

VHS rentals, due dates, member cards, and tapes nobody returned on time.

Lucky 8 Gas & Motel

Cash paid after midnight.

Full tanks, room keys, road maps, and one-night stays along a highway that kept moving past.

Side B Records

Basement finds and last copies.

Used tapes, store credit, handwritten catalog numbers, and something still humming under the static.

Sunset Mart

Ordinary nights, small totals.

Cola, matches, mints, lost change, and receipts from moments that felt too small to save.

Token Pawn

Items held for thirty days.

Broken watches, arcade tokens, ring boxes, claim checks, and things nobody came back to collect.

Manifesto

Small Proofs Survive

Most things disappear quietly.

A store closes. A sign comes down. A road gets renamed. A register is unplugged. A tape is thrown away. A room key is lost. Someone moves on.

But sometimes one small piece of paper survives. A receipt does not explain love, regret, disappearance, or why someone never came back. It only prints the facts.

Date. Time. Item. Total. Paid cash.

That is enough.

No Refunds Archive exists for the things that were too small to become history, but too personal to be forgotten.
